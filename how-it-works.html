Physics-informed sequence analysis that predicts synthesis stability and operational reliability

Core Validation Metrics

Symbolic Entropy (Ssym)

Definition: A unitless measure quantifying the thermodynamic disorder and information content of a DNA sequence structure.

Interpretation:

  • Low Ssym (<0.3): Highly ordered, stable structure - reliable synthesis expected
  • Medium Ssym (0.3-0.7): Moderate stability - synthesis possible with careful conditions
  • High Ssym (>0.7): Disordered, unstable - high synthesis failure risk

What it captures: Ssym integrates multiple thermodynamic properties including base-pairing stability, secondary structure complexity, torsional strain, and temperature sensitivity. It correlates with synthesis success rates, replication fidelity, and operational reliability.

Example: In validation studies, sequences with Ssym < 0.4 showed 92% synthesis success, while sequences with Ssym > 0.6 showed only 31% success.

Torsion Field Profile

Definition: Position-dependent measurement of structural stress and mechanical instability along the sequence.

Interpretation:

  • Low torsion regions: Stable structural motifs
  • High torsion regions: "Hotspots" prone to synthesis errors, replication mistakes, or functional failures
  • Sharp torsion gradients: Structural transition zones requiring careful design

What it captures: Torsion profiles reveal where sequences experience mechanical stress during synthesis, replication, and operation. Hotspots correlate with polymerase stalling, secondary structure formation, and GC-rich clustering problems.

Example: A 500bp sequence might show 3-4 torsion hotspots. Optimizing just these regions (15-20% of total length) can improve overall synthesis success from 45% to 85%.

Compliance Score

Definition: Composite metric (0-1 scale) combining Ssym, torsion analysis, and sequence-specific factors into a single synthesis-readiness score.

Interpretation:

  • Score > 0.8: Stable - Proceed with synthesis
  • Score 0.5-0.8: Marginal - Consider optimization or special handling
  • Score < 0.5: Unstable - Redesign recommended before synthesis

What it captures: The compliance score integrates global stability (Ssym), local instabilities (torsion hotspots), sequence complexity, repeat structures, and empirical success predictors into a single actionable metric.

Example: Pre-screening with compliance scores > 0.7 reduces synthesis attempts on problematic sequences, cutting failure rates from 25-30% to 5-8% in pilot programs.

Technical Approach

Thermodynamic Field Analysis

Œ¶-mapping treats DNA sequences as one-dimensional thermodynamic systems with spatially-varying properties. Unlike sequence-only analysis (GC content, repeat detection) or purely structural prediction (folding algorithms), Œ¶-mapping models the energy landscape and mechanical stress distribution throughout the sequence.

The approach combines:

  • Statistical mechanics: Entropy calculations based on accessible microstates
  • Continuum mechanics: Torsional stress modeling for structural instability
  • Information theory: Symbolic complexity measures for sequence organization
  • Empirical calibration: Machine learning on validated synthesis datasets

Computational Implementation

The Œ¶-mapping engine is implemented in optimized Python with C++ acceleration for performance-critical components. Key features:

  • Fast computation: Typical analysis <1 second for sequences up to 10kb
  • Scalable: Batch processing of thousands of sequences using parallel computation
  • GPU acceleration: Optional CUDA support for large-scale analysis
  • Validated algorithms: Published methods with peer-reviewed validation

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Œ¶-mapping different from existing sequence analysis tools?

Traditional tools focus on sequence composition (GC content, repeats) or structural prediction (folding, secondary structure). Œ¶-mapping adds thermodynamic analysis that predicts synthesis stability and operational reliability. It's complementary to existing tools, not a replacement.

Do I need to change my design workflow?

No. Œ¶-mapping integrates as a validation step in your existing workflow. You can use it to pre-screen designs before synthesis, analyze failures after the fact, or both. It works with sequences from any design tool.

What sequence types are supported?

Œ¶-mapping works with any DNA sequence: genes, plasmids, constructs, primers, libraries, or full genomes. It handles both natural and synthetic sequences. Recommended length: 50bp to 50kbp, though longer sequences are supported.

How accurate are the predictions?

Validation studies show R¬≤ = 0.83 correlation between Œ¶-scores and synthesis success rates, with >80% classification accuracy. Accuracy improves when calibrated to specific synthesis platforms and conditions.

Can Œ¶-mapping suggest sequence improvements?

Currently, Œ¶-mapping identifies problematic regions (hotspots) and classifies stability. It doesn't yet provide automated redesign suggestions, but pinpointing problematic regions guides manual optimization. Automated optimization is on the roadmap.

Is my sequence data kept private?

Yes. Cloud API data is encrypted in transit and at rest, never shared, and deleted after 30 days. For maximum privacy, use the on-premises Docker option where no data leaves your infrastructure.

What does a pilot program involve?

Typical pilot: (1) Retrospective analysis on your historical data, (2) Prospective validation on new sequences, (3) Measure ROI and accuracy, (4) Optional co-publication. Duration: 3-6 months. Cost: Free during validation phase.
